# Judging Rubric

CYVL Physical AI Hackathon. Four criteria, equally weighted, 25% each. Total score out of 16.

The official scoring sheet uses these four. The kickoff deck names them slightly differently (in parentheses); same four things.

## 1. Business Strength (/4)  — deck: "Business Strength"

- 0: no clear customer, problem, or value.
- 4: real buyer, clear value, credible path to market.

A startup you could start now, not in five years.

## 2. Technical Innovation & Execution (/4)  — deck: "Technical Excellence"

- 0: generic software that already exists (a Google Maps clone) or doesn't work.
- 4: could scale, functions well, nothing hidden in the demo, feels like it is already in production.

## 3. Use of CYVL & Sponsor Data / Application (/4)  — deck: "Partner Tech in the Stack"

- 0: data unused or fake data.
- 4: CYVL / sponsor data is central to how the product works.

Real use of a couple: Cyvl, Autodesk, NVIDIA, Boston MCP. More is not better.

## 4. Presentation (/4)  — deck: "The Presentation"

- 0: unclear story, no demo, unprepared.
- 4: clear, confident pitch and demo within time.

Tell it well, at the booth and on the big screen.

## How to read this

- Real Cyvl/sponsor data has to be **central**, not decorated on top. Fake data scores a 0 on a quarter of the total.
- "Google Maps clone" is called out by name as a 0. Do something Maps cannot.
- Working demo matters as much as the pitch. Nothing faked, nothing hidden.
- Pitch within the time limit: 5 minutes plus 2 for questions (see [HACKATHON.md](HACKATHON.md)).

## Quick self-check before judging

- Who is the buyer, and would they pay? (Business)
- Does it run live, end to end, scale plausibly? (Technical)
- Is Cyvl/sponsor data load-bearing? Remove it and does the product die? (Data)
- Can you tell the whole story in 5 minutes with a clean demo? (Presentation)
