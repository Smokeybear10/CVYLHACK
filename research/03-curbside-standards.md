# Curbside / On-Street EV Charging Siting Standards, Clearances & Municipal Checklists

Research for **Sonder** — curb-segment screening & rating for curbside Level 2 EV charging in Somerville, MA.
Compiled 2026-06-13. All distances are quoted from primary sources; source URL given inline.

---

## TL;DR — the rules that matter most for screening a curb segment

The single most concrete, copy-able rule set in the US is **Portland PBOT's TRN-10.19 administrative rule** (adopted from its Feb/Mar 2023 "EV Charging in the Public Right-of-Way Code Project"). It gives hard, numeric setbacks for nearly every street feature. Treat it as Sonder's default clearance schedule and adjust for Somerville/MA. The **US Access Board** EV guidance (July 2022) governs ADA. The **NYC "Curb Enthusiasm"** report (NYSERDA/NYSDOT, 2019) and **Seattle EVCROW** give the planning-level "avoid" lists. **Somerville's own Cadmus siting report (2020)** + the **SomerVoice on-street program** give the local scoring logic.

## 1. Master Clearance / Setback Table

Distances are **minimums** unless noted. The Portland column is the most authoritative published numeric set; other cities are sparser and are noted where they differ.

| Street feature | Required clearance | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Intersection / corner** | **25 ft**, measured from the back of the sidewalk corridor | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(i) |
| **Stop sign** | **20 ft** | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(iii) |
| **Alley or driveway (curb cut)** | **5 ft**, measured from the curb cut | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(ii) |
| **Fire hydrant** | **10 ft** | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(iv) |
| **Other water-utility infrastructure** | **5 ft** | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(v) |
| **Bus stop / MAX or streetcar transit platform / transit lift stop** | **30 ft**, measured from the bus-stop sign, counter to traffic flow | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(ix) |
| **Bike-share docking station (each end)** | **5 ft** | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(vi) |
| **Bike rack (each end)** | **3 ft** (rack may be relocated at PBOT discretion) | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(vii) |
| **Light pole / utility pole / traffic signal** (excluding a pole the charger is mounted on) | **3 ft** | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(a)(viii), §5(a) |
| **Pedestrian Through Zone (clear width to preserve)** | **3 ft minimum** in all scenarios; **6 ft encouraged** when feasible | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(b); US Access Board (see §3) |
| **Property line (alignment target)** | within **5 ft** of the property line extended, when possible | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(c) |
| **Charger face distance to curb** | **as close to curb as possible, no farther than 10 in** from the face of the curb | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(d)(iii) |
| **"Middle 50%" of the adjacent parallel space** | charger must **NOT** sit in the middle 50% (would block vehicle entry/exit) — bias to ends of the space / end of block | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(d)(iii); US Access Board |
| **ADA ground space at charger** | **30 in × 48 in** minimum, free of grass/curbs/wheel stops/bollards, firm/stable/slip-resistant | Portland TRN-10.19 §3(d)(v); US Access Board |
| **Streetcar lines / transit operations / roadway / bike lane** | charger may **NOT** be on a streetcar street, impede transit, or protrude into a roadway or bike lane (no numeric setback — exclusion) | Portland TRN-10.19 §2(b) |
| **Stormwater planters / swales / maintenance holes / handholes** | charger may **NOT** be located in/over them | Portland TRN-10.19 §5(c)(d) |

### Other cities' published exclusions (planning-level, mostly non-numeric)

- **NYC (Curb Enthusiasm, NYSDOT/NYSERDA 2019, §9.1 #14):** AVOID — transit-priority streets (current/planned), locations that interfere with bus layovers, locations adjacent to **protected bike lanes**, blocks with frequent street closures (festivals), peak-period parking-restricted blocks, and historic districts / near registered landmarks (triggers extra design review). Prioritize **metered blocks**. ([source](https://www.dot.ny.gov/divisions/engineering/technical-services/trans-r-and-d-repository/C-17-06_Curb_Enthusiasm_Final_Report.pdf))
- **Seattle EVCROW:** must have existing street parking, be within city limits, and **must not restrict or block existing or planned bike or transit lanes**. ([source](https://www.seattle.gov/city-light/in-the-community/current-projects/curbside-level-2-ev-charging))
- **LA (LADWP/LADOT):** street-light-pole-mounted chargers; department **favors side streets over main avenues** to avoid friction over loss of retail-frontage parking. ([source](https://www.dot.ny.gov/divisions/engineering/technical-services/trans-r-and-d-repository/C-17-06_Curb_Enthusiasm_Final_Report.pdf), Curb Enthusiasm §2.1.1)

## 2. Sidewalk Geometry & Zones (where the charger and cord may go)

Curbside EVSE belongs in the **Furnishing Zone** (a.k.a. street-furniture / amenity zone) — the band between the curb and the pedestrian through-zone — alongside trees, signal/utility poles, signs, hydrants, parking meters, bike parking. It must **not** intrude on the Pedestrian Through Zone. (Portland Pedestrian Design Guide three-zone model: Frontage Zone → Pedestrian Through Zone → Furnishing Zone → Curb Zone.) ([Portland code project report, p.13](https://www.portland.gov/transportation/electric-vehicles/ev-charging-row); [NACTO Sidewalk Zones](https://nacto.org/publication/urban-street-design-guide/street-design-elements/sidewalks/sidewalk-zones/))

**Pedestrian Through-Zone minimum clear widths:**
- **Portland rule:** ≥ **3 ft** maintained in all cases; **6 ft preferred**. (Portland TRN-10.19 §3(b))
- **NACTO guidance:** through zone should be **5–7 ft** in residential settings, **8–12 ft** in downtown/commercial. ([NACTO](https://nacto.org/publication/urban-street-design-guide/street-design-elements/sidewalks/sidewalk-zones/))
- **Seattle City Light curbside L2 program (physical eligibility):** sidewalk must be **≥ 11.5 ft wide** and the parking space **8 ft wide** — most Seattle curbs are narrower, which has been the program's biggest constraint. ([Powerlines / E&E](https://www.eenews.net/articles/seattle-shows-why-curbside-charging-is-so-hard/))

**Charger placement detail (Portland TRN-10.19 §3(d)):**
- Located on a block face with an **ADA-compliant curb ramp**.
- Must not impede pedestrian passage or ADA access (3 ft min through-zone, 6 ft preferred).
- Installed **≤ 10 in from the face of the curb**.
- Oriented so the **critical operable parts do NOT face the street/curb** (so the user operates it from the sidewalk side).
- Surrounded by **30 in × 48 in** clear ground space (no grass/curb/wheel stop/bollard inside it); additional clear space for forward approach + turning when feasible.

**Vertical clearance:** Pole-mounted units in LA are mounted **9 ft above the sidewalk** on existing street-light poles (vandalism protection + pedestrian clearance). ([EV Engineering](https://www.evengineeringonline.com/bringing-accessible-streetlight-ev-charging-to-la/)) Overhead cross-sidewalk cord systems (Cambridge) require **min. 9 ft aerial clearance**. ([Berkeley Law overview](https://www.law.berkeley.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Overview_-City-programs-allowing-residential-EV-charging-cords-across-the-public-right-of-way.pdf))

## 3. ADA / Accessibility for Curbside Charging

Primary federal source: **U.S. Access Board, "Design Recommendations for Accessible Electric Vehicle Charging Stations," July 2022**, plus the **Sept 2024 NPRM** (proposed ADA/ABA accessibility guidelines for EV charging). On-street EV charging currently has **no binding federal percentage requirement**; the guidance is recommendations + the NPRM scoping table. ([Access Board guidance](https://www.access-board.gov/tad/ev/); [Federal Register NPRM](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/09/03/2024-18820/americans-with-disabilities-act-and-architectural-barriers-act-accessibility-guidelines-ev-charging))

**Key dimensions (Access Board):**
- **Clear floor/ground space at the charger:** **30 in × 48 in** minimum (parallel approach). Operable parts no farther than **10 in** from the space.
- **Operable-parts reach range:** high side reach **≤ 48 in** above ground; low side reach **≥ 15 in**. Screen viewable at **40 in** above ground.
- **Operation force:** usable with one hand, no tight grasping/pinching/twisting, **≤ 5 lbf**.
- **Vehicle charging space (off-street model):** **11 ft wide × 20 ft long** (132 in × 240 in), height clearance 98 in; **pull-through** spaces **≥ 16 ft wide** with cable reach to a vehicle 5 ft away.
- **Access aisle:** **≥ 5 ft (60 in) wide**, full length of the charging space; two spaces may share one aisle. Charging space + access aisle level, slope **≤ 1:48**.
- **Surface:** stable, firm, slip-resistant; no level changes between space and aisle.

**Proposed scoping ratio (NPRM, per charger type at a station):** **1–25 chargers → 1 accessible; 26–50 → 2**, computed separately for each charger type. The 2022 guidance recommends **at least 2** chargers with accessible mobility features at a station. *All* chargers (not just accessible ones) need volume control, visual/tactile status indicators, non-color communication, and audible+visual/tactile signals. ([ADA Title III summary](https://www.adatitleiii.com/2024/09/u-s-access-board-issues-proposed-rule-for-accessible-electric-vehicle-charging-stations/))

**On-street specific (Access Board):** position the accessible curbside charger at the **end of the block, or at the closest curb ramp**, rather than mid-block / in the middle 50% of parking spaces. Portland operationalizes this: block face must have an ADA-compliant curb ramp; permittee may have to build accessibility improvements.

## 4. On-Street Parking-Space Dimensions for Charging

- **Portland "EV Charging Only" space (Parking Management Manual draft):** **≥ 8 ft wide × 20 ft long**, painted with white outline stripes and the words **"EV only"** inside the space. ([Portland code project report, Appendix D](https://www.portland.gov/transportation/electric-vehicles/ev-charging-row))
- **Seattle City Light eligibility:** parking space **8 ft wide** (with the 11.5 ft sidewalk). ([E&E](https://www.eenews.net/articles/seattle-shows-why-curbside-charging-is-so-hard/))
- **Access Board accessible space (off-street):** 11 ft × 20 ft + 5 ft aisle (see §3).
- **Somerville:** allows **parallel, 45°, or 90°** parking orientations for on-street chargers. ([SomerVoice FAQ](https://voice.somervillema.gov/on-street-ev-charging/news_feed/on-street-ev-charging-faqs))
- General on-street parallel reference: a standard parallel parking stall is typically ~**8 ft × 20–22 ft**; charger should be placed so the cord can reach **either driver- or passenger-side ports** in a parallel deployment (LADWP cited the lack of an industry charge-port-location standard as a real siting headache — cord length must cover both). (Curb Enthusiasm §2.1.1)

## 5. Mounting: Pole vs. Pedestal & Cord / Trip-Hazard Rules

**Mounting:**
- **Pole-mounted on existing utility/street-light poles** is the cheapest and least ROW-intrusive: no asphalt cutting, no new furnishing-zone footprint. Used by **LA/LADWP** (82 of 85 units on street-light poles, 2 on wood power poles, 1 pedestal DCFC) and **Portland/PGE** (utility-pole pilot at SE Clinton). Portland's rule explicitly allows mounting on a utility pole and exempts that pole from the 3 ft pole setback. ([Curb Enthusiasm §2.1.1](https://www.dot.ny.gov/divisions/engineering/technical-services/trans-r-and-d-repository/C-17-06_Curb_Enthusiasm_Final_Report.pdf); Portland TRN-10.19)
- **Pedestal** ("roughly the size of a parking meter / Parking Kitty"): used where no suitable pole exists; sits in the furnishing zone. Boston/Cambridge "itselectric" and Flo curbside units are low-profile pedestals "similar to a parking meter." ([Boston.gov](https://www.boston.gov/departments/transportation/curbside-ev-charging))
- **Pole caveat (LADWP):** shared poles created operational conflicts — linemen / cable / telecom need retraining and protocols to work around EVSE; secure utility buy-in before scaling. (Curb Enthusiasm §2.1.1)

**Cord management / trip hazard:**
- **NYC rule (Curb Enthusiasm §9.4):** infrastructure must "deploy a cord management system that minimizes pedestrian trip hazard"; be UL-certified, PDC-approved, fit NYCDOT's approved street-furniture family, be vandal-proof and weather-operable, and metered.
- **Detachable-cable model (Boston/Cambridge itselectric):** drivers carry their own detachable cable so **no cable is in the ROW when no car is charging** — well suited to narrow historic streets. ([Boston.gov](https://www.boston.gov/departments/transportation/curbside-ev-charging))
- **Cross-sidewalk cord (residential L1 only) — Seattle SDOT CAM 2119** (the most precise published cord-ramp spec):
  - Cord must cross **perpendicular** to the sidewalk.
  - **Only Level 1 (110–120 V)**; **no Level 2 (240 V) cords across the ROW**.
  - Cable ramp / cover required: highly visible, stable, secure, low-angle.
  - If total cord+ramp height **≤ ½ in**: ramp covers cord fully across the sidewalk, **≥ 4 ft long**, slope **≤ 50% grade (1:2)**.
  - If height **> ½ in**: ramp slope **≤ 8.3% (1:12)**; **3 ft × 5 ft clear landing on each side**; ramp covers full sidewalk width, **≥ 4 ft wide**; **5 ft × 4 ft+ landing platform** at top; landing slope **≤ 2% (1:50)** any direction; cross-slope **≤ 2%**; preserve **5 ft of clear sidewalk** on each side.
  - Equipment removed when not charging; outlet on owner's utility account; NEC-compliant; no reserving the space / no signage.
  - ([CAM 2119 PDF](https://seattle.gov/Documents/Departments/SDOT/CAMs/CAM2119.pdf))
- **Washington DC (DDOT cord guidance):** L1 only across ROW; cord **perpendicular** to sidewalk; covered by a highly visible low-angle cable ramp (reflective tape encouraged for night); equipment removed when not charging; no public-space permit needed if guidance followed. Owner "should" carry adequate insurance (**$1M suggested**); equipment not fastened in place must not exceed **80% of branch-circuit ampere rating**. ([Berkeley Law overview](https://www.law.berkeley.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Overview_-City-programs-allowing-residential-EV-charging-cords-across-the-public-right-of-way.pdf))
- **Cambridge, MA cross-sidewalk program** (directly adjacent to Somerville): DPW pilot, **annual permit $200**, L1 only, sidewalk cord-cover OR overhead (overhead needs **9 ft clearance** + retractable arm onto private property); ramp must be ADA-compliant for ½ in and 1 in cords; in place **only while charging, 12 hr max**; applicant must hold a residential parking permit + show homeowner/renter insurance + electrician's certification. ([Berkeley Law overview](https://www.law.berkeley.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Overview_-City-programs-allowing-residential-EV-charging-cords-across-the-public-right-of-way.pdf); [Boston Globe](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2023/08/21/business/cambridge-legalizes-ev-charging-across-sidewalk/))

> Note: cross-sidewalk-cord programs are **residential L1**, a different product from Sonder's **public curbside L2 pedestal/pole** model. They matter for Sonder mainly as the ADA cord-geometry reference and the local (Cambridge/DC) precedent that L2 cords across a public sidewalk are uniformly prohibited.

## 6. Spacing, Density & Turnover

- **Portland:** EV chargers should, when feasible, charge **two vehicles simultaneously** (build "charging pods"); EV-Only spaces are **4-hour time-limited** during managed-parking hours (default 8 am–6 pm where unmanaged); wayfinding signage at the **nearest intersection in both travel directions**. ([Portland code project, Appendix D](https://www.portland.gov/transportation/electric-vehicles/ev-charging-row))
- **NYC (Curb Enthusiasm §9.1):** "Limit the number of Level 2 clustered in one NTA [neighborhood] until there is proven demand." Site on **metered blocks** for daytime turnover. NYCDOT pilot = up to **60 dual-port** L2 stations citywide. Avoid blocks with **weekly alternate-side parking** (a single BEV could occupy a space for a week).
- **Boston/Flo curbside:** **4-hour limit** daytime, **no limit overnight** (10 pm–8 am or 6 pm–8 am depending on block). ([Boston.gov](https://www.boston.gov/departments/transportation/curbside-ev-charging))
- **Somerville coverage target:** Cadmus modeled that **~5 well-targeted stations** could let "the vast majority of Somerville residents walk to an EV charger within **half a mile** of their residence." ([Cadmus report, p.19](https://s3.amazonaws.com/somervillema-live/s3fs-public/EVCharging%20Report_Final.pdf))

## 7. City Program Snapshots (siting criteria)

**NYC DOT Curbside Level 2 Pilot** — sites chosen by projected demand, geographic diversity, equity (deliberately including low/moderate-income, low-EV neighborhoods), elected-official + community-board input, 311/online portal requests. Design per **NYCDOT Street Design Manual, MUTCD, AASHTO Geometric Design, ADA Standards**. Prioritize sites **near an electrical panel** (trenching kills feasibility). Sign as "electric vehicle parking while charging only"; tow non-EVs / idle EVs. ([Curb Enthusiasm §9](https://www.dot.ny.gov/divisions/engineering/technical-services/trans-r-and-d-repository/C-17-06_Curb_Enthusiasm_Final_Report.pdf); [NYC pilot report](https://www.nyc.gov/html/dot/downloads/pdf/curbside-level-2-charging-pilot-evaluation-report.pdf))

**Portland PBOT EV-in-ROW** — only **vendors with a city franchise/privilege** (not individual residents) may install; **L2 only** (208–240 V), no DCFC; only in **Gateway Regional / Town / Neighborhood Centers** and only on **Local Service Traffic Streets** (side streets "around the corner" from arterials), on a **city-maintained street with a curb**, where parking is currently allowed. Full numeric clearance schedule in §1. Design-exception process for sites that can't meet a rule. ([Portland code project + TRN-10.19 appendix](https://www.portland.gov/transportation/electric-vehicles/ev-charging-row))

**Seattle EVCROW (City Light)** — utility-owned curbside L2 network targeting dense, transit-rich "urban centers/villages," low EV/EVSE penetration, poor-air-quality areas; needs existing street parking, can't block bike/transit lanes; sidewalk ≥ 11.5 ft & space 8 ft; SDOT pre-approves street-use permits; guidance on street-tree protection, lighting, ADA, metering. ([Curb Enthusiasm §2.1.2](https://www.dot.ny.gov/divisions/engineering/technical-services/trans-r-and-d-repository/C-17-06_Curb_Enthusiasm_Final_Report.pdf); [Seattle program page](https://www.seattle.gov/city-light/in-the-community/current-projects/curbside-level-2-ev-charging))

**LA (LADWP/LADOT + LABSL)** — street-light-pole-mounted L2; chargers ~**9 ft up** the pole; prefer **side streets**; cord must reach driver- or passenger-side port; weighing meter-removal vs. revenue-sharing (~$30k/yr/meter). ([Curb Enthusiasm §2.1.1](https://www.dot.ny.gov/divisions/engineering/technical-services/trans-r-and-d-repository/C-17-06_Curb_Enthusiasm_Final_Report.pdf); [LA BSL](https://lalights.lacity.org/connected-infrastructure/ev_stations.html))

**Boston** — 96 curbside ports at 24 locations (Flo CoRe+ MAX L2, up to 19.2 kW / 80 A, J1772); low-profile, "like a parking meter"; sited adjacent to public amenities (parks, libraries), generally **not adjacent to residential property** unless residents requested; detachable cables (no ROW cable when idle); 4-hr day limit. ([Boston.gov curbside](https://www.boston.gov/departments/transportation/curbside-ev-charging))

**Washington DC (DDOT)** — Neighborhood Curbside EV Charging Pilot running through 2026 to set permanent vendor rules; current binding rule is the **cross-sidewalk L1 cord guidance** (perpendicular, cable ramp, no L2). ([DDOT program](https://ddot.dc.gov/page/electric-vehicle-charging-station-program); [DDOT release](https://ddot.dc.gov/release/ddot-announces-neighborhood-curbside-electric-vehicle-ev-charging-station-pilot))

**Somerville, MA** (Sonder's actual city) — On-street public **L2** program (ChargePoint CT4000/CP6000), $0.25/kWh (as of 7/2024); 10 sites evaluated 2025, 6 selected on **electrical-install feasibility, neighborhoods with limited driveway charging, cost, community preference**. Cadmus 2020 siting methodology scored census block groups on (in priority order): **(1) least existing L2 access, (2) MA Environmental Justice Community tier, (3) multifamily-dwelling density, (4) car-commuting share** → composite 0.00–0.88. It **removed transit-priority corridors Broadway, Elm, and Holland** from curbside consideration; allocated chargers across metered curbs, 2-hour spaces, public lots, and spaces adjacent to city land; flagged that detailed siting must still check **street trees, business parking permits, bus stops, curb cuts, bike share, and electric distribution capacity**. ([Somerville Cadmus report](https://s3.amazonaws.com/somervillema-live/s3fs-public/EVCharging%20Report_Final.pdf); [SomerVoice on-street EV](https://voice.somervillema.gov/on-street-ev-charging); [FAQ](https://voice.somervillema.gov/on-street-ev-charging/news_feed/on-street-ev-charging-faqs))

## 8. Mapping to Sonder — what we can auto-check vs. must flag

Sonder rates a curb segment from detected street assets + imagery. Below: which rules are **machine-checkable from detected geometry/assets**, which need **GIS overlays**, and which must be **flagged for human/field review**.

### A. Directly checkable from detected point assets + curb geometry (high confidence)
| Rule | How Sonder checks it |
|---|---|
| **Fire hydrant ≥ 10 ft** | Detect hydrant → buffer 10 ft → reject overlapping curb. (Use 10 ft Portland; MA fire code commonly cites larger no-park zones — see flags.) |
| **Driveway / curb cut ≥ 5 ft** | Detect curb cuts/driveways from imagery → 5 ft buffer. |
| **Intersection / corner ≥ 25 ft** | From street centerline graph → 25 ft from intersection along curb. |
| **Stop sign ≥ 20 ft** | Detect stop sign (MUTCD R1-1) → 20 ft buffer. |
| **Bus stop ≥ 30 ft** | Detect bus-stop sign/shelter → 30 ft buffer. |
| **Light/utility/signal pole ≥ 3 ft** (3 ft, unless mounting on it) | Detect poles; if pole-mount candidate, exempt that pole. |
| **Charger ≤ 10 in from curb / furnishing zone exists** | Need a furnishing zone of usable depth at the curb. |
| **Bike rack ≥ 3 ft, bike-share dock ≥ 5 ft** | Detect bike racks / docking stations → buffer. |
| **Curb ramp on the block face (ADA)** | Detect curb ramps; require ≥1 ADA-compliant ramp on the block, ideally place charger near it / at block end. |
| **Charger NOT in middle 50% of the parallel space** | Geometric placement check once a target stall is chosen. |

### B. Checkable only with a GIS/policy overlay (medium confidence — needs data layer, not just imagery)
| Rule | Layer needed |
|---|---|
| Pedestrian Through Zone ≥ 3 ft (6 ft pref.) / sidewalk width | Sidewalk-width layer or measured-from-imagery curb-to-building distance (Seattle's 11.5 ft test). |
| Not on a transit-priority / streetcar / protected-bike-lane street | City bike-network + transit-priority street layers (Somerville: exclude **Broadway, Elm, Holland**). |
| Local Service Traffic Street vs. arterial (Portland-style) | Street-classification layer. |
| Parking currently allowed; not a peak-restricted / alternate-side block | Parking-regulation layer (metered vs. 2-hr vs. no-park). Somerville mapped metered curbs / 2-hr spaces / lots. |
| Near electrical panel / distribution capacity | Utility infrastructure (often unavailable → flag). |
| Historic district / landmark (extra design review) | Historic-district layer. |
| Bus layover zones, loading/no-standing zones | Curb-regulation layer (often the binding constraint; frequently missing). |
| Stormwater planters / swales | Green-infrastructure layer. |

### C. Must be FLAGGED for human / field review (cannot resolve from current detection)
- **Exact MA / Somerville fire-hydrant no-park distance** (MA practice and local ordinance can exceed Portland's 10 ft; MUTCD/local often use ~15 ft). Treat 10 ft as a floor and flag for code confirmation.
- **Vertical & overhead conditions** (tree canopy, awnings, wires) for pole-mount feasibility and the LA 9 ft mounting height.
- **Street-tree / root conflict** — Portland requires City Forester sign-off; detect trees but flag conflicts.
- **Electrical distribution capacity / nearest transformer or panel** — drives cost more than geometry; almost never in asset data.
- **Cord reach to both vehicle ports** for the specific parallel stall (no industry port-location standard).
- **Snow-removal / plow exposure** and seasonal alternate-side parking suspensions (NYC §9.5).
- **ADA operable-parts, reach range, and clear-ground-surface firmness** (30×48 in) — placement is checkable, surface compliance needs field verification.
- **Loading zones / no-standing / bus-layover curb regulations** if no curb-regulation GIS layer exists.

### Suggested Sonder scoring rubric (synthesized)
1. **Hard pass/fail (binary):** hydrant, driveway, intersection, stop sign, bus stop, bike facility setbacks; furnishing zone present; ADA through-zone ≥ 3 ft; not in roadway/bike lane.
2. **Weighted positive signals** (from NYC/Seattle/Somerville logic): metered or time-limited block (turnover), Local Service / side street, near an ADA curb ramp, in an EJ / multifamily / low-existing-access block group, ≥ ½-mile coverage gap, adjacent to existing utility pole (cheap mount).
3. **Weighted negatives / flags:** transit-priority corridor, protected bike lane, alternate-side parking, historic district, on Broadway/Elm/Holland (Somerville), unknown electrical capacity.
